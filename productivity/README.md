### [AppFlowy](https://github.com/AppFlowy-IO/AppFlowy) ⭐ 69.1k

**Self-hosted Notion alternative — AI-powered workspace built with Flutter and Rust.**

AppFlowy is an open-source collaborative workspace: rich document editing, grid databases, kanban boards, calendar views, and team wikis — all in one cross-platform app. Built with Flutter (UI) and Rust (backend/local storage), it ships native desktop apps for macOS, Windows, and Linux, plus mobile apps for iOS and Android. Self-host the sync server for teams, or run locally without any server at all. AI assistant built in, powered by the LLM of your choice.

**Stack:** Flutter, Rust, self-hosted sync server
**License:** AGPL-3.0

**Why it's notable:** Notion became the default collaborative workspace for a generation of teams, then introduced pricing cliffs, opaque AI add-ons, and terms that make data portability uncomfortable. AppFlowy's answer is principled: local-first data ownership, open source, native performance. 69.1k stars and a last commit on April 5, 2026 (today) signals a project at peak momentum. The Flutter + Rust architecture choice is a long-term bet on performance and cross-platform consistency — the codebase is designed to outlast Electron-based competitors.

### [Rowboat](https://github.com/rowboatlabs/rowboat) ⭐ 10k

**Local-first AI coworker that turns your emails and meetings into a living knowledge graph — and acts on it.**

Rowboat connects to your communications and calendar, ingests everything into an editable Markdown vault, and builds a persistent knowledge graph from your actual work history. From that context, it generates PDFs, briefs, and draft emails grounded in what you've actually said and decided — not hallucinated from thin air. "Live notes" update automatically as new information comes in. MCP integration for external tools, works with any local or hosted LLM, optional voice input/output. The knowledge graph is fully transparent: it's Markdown on disk, readable and editable at any time.

**Stack:** TypeScript
**License:** Apache-2.0

**Why it's notable:** Most AI assistants are stateless — every conversation starts cold. Rowboat's core insight is that the bottleneck isn't the model, it's context: your AI coworker needs to know what happened in last Tuesday's meeting before it can draft Friday's brief. By building a persistent, editable knowledge graph from real communications rather than requiring manual note-taking, Rowboat closes that gap. The local-first architecture means your email and meeting data never touches a vendor's servers. 10k stars and a commit on April 8, 2026 signals strong early traction for a tool solving a problem every knowledge worker has.
